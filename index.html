Getting started on this site:


There are two basic components to this site:
  1. Example pages - These are most of the pages on the site, including the product page, product detail pages, cart, and confirmation page. This is where you should be practicing your pixel implementation.

  2. Help page - This page will include more information about the site, practice problems, prompts, sitemap files, and more. Click here or clicking the Help button on the top menu. You can always return to that page at any time to check back in from the menu button.
Don't hesitate to reach out to the Pixel COE on Slack (#pixels) if you have any questions!

Tracking Implementation Explanation:


On this site, we've added a user indentifying first-party cookie called "KG_Sandbox_ID." We log site interactions in database (Google Sheet) to help show how identifiers and actions can be used for audience segmentation. Don't worry, we don't have any way to figure out "who" is tied to an ID.

For your browser, we've assigned the ID:


We keep this cookie stored in your browser for 30 days, so if you come back from the same browser and haven't cleared your cookies, we'll keep using the same ID.

If this is the first time you've visited, you're "undefined," but we just set a new cookie with a unique(ish) number in your browser's storage. If you refresh your page, or whenever you return for the next 30 days, you'll see your browser's identifier below. Click here, or refresh the page to see what ID we've set.

Cookies can be deleted. This happens automatically for some users if they prefer browsers that have these features, such as ITP in Safari, and ETP in Firefox. For other users, they may actually manually delete their cookies to increase their anonymity to the ad tech community. When they delete their cookies, the next time they visit a site, a new ID will be created. If you want to test this out, take note of the ID above, and then click the button below. When your page reloads, you'll now see your ID as undefined, and a new ID will be set and used for the rest of your browsing.
